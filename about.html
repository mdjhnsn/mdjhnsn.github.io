Who I Am

I'm a Senior Data Scientist with 10 years of experience building production ML systems for platforms serving 100M+ users. My background combines econometric rigor (MS Statistics, MA Economics) with hands-on production ML expertise—from revenue forecasting that drives financial planning to data infrastructure that ensures 24/7 system reliability.

What makes my approach unique is the intersection of three things:

Statistical Rigor — My training in Statistics and Economics gives me the causal inference mindset that marketing measurement and experimentation demand. I instinctively ask "what variation in the data identifies this effect?" and "could this be confounded?"

Production ML at Scale — I've built systems that process 50TB+ monthly, serve predictions for 100M+ users, and handle hundreds of millions of events daily. I understand both the statistical theory and the operational reality of deploying models that businesses rely on.

Communication & Mentorship — Before transitioning to data science, I taught English in Japan for four years. That experience fundamentally shaped how I communicate technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders and mentor junior data scientists—I know how to break down complex ideas and adapt my teaching style to different learning preferences.

My Philosophy

"Decision Owner" Mindset

I don't just build models—I own the decisions those models enable. Whether it's revenue forecasts serving as the primary input for executive financial planning or churn predictions driving retention strategy, I ensure the systems I build are reliable enough for leadership to stake real business outcomes on them.

Triangulation Over Single Methods

In marketing measurement, no single methodology provides ground truth. I believe in triangulation: using incrementality experiments to calibrate MMM priors, while MTA provides tactical speed for daily optimization. This philosophy extends to all my work—multiple validation approaches, uncertainty quantification, and healthy skepticism of any single metric.

GenAI as Productivity Multiplier

I use GenAI coding tools (Cursor, Copilot, LLM APIs) daily as productivity multipliers. They've become integral to how I work: rapid prototyping, code review, documentation generation, and exploring unfamiliar codebases. I also mentor team members on effective AI-assisted development practices.
